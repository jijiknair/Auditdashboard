AUDIT DASHBOARD

Welcome to the Infection Prevention and Control (IPC) Audit section of the Hospital Audit Dashboard. This tool has been designed to help auditors conduct thorough and efficient audits of infection control practices within healthcare facilities. Your role as an auditor is essential in ensuring that the highest standards of hygiene, safety, and infection prevention are maintained. Please proceed carefully through each section and provide accurate, timely feedback.

Purpose: To monitor and optimize infection prevention and control measures in primary healthcare facilities, and to prevent healthcare-associated infections (HAIs).

The IPC Audit worksheet for Primary Healthcare Facilities has been designed to assist health facilities in auditing infection prevention and control practices, recording the results, and developing action plans if necessary. The format of the checklists is user-friendly, with risk-based scoring criteria. This worksheet contains an audit score sheet for IPC practices and an auto-generated report for conducted audits.

Each primary healthcare facility should be audited at least annually. The frequency of audits depends on the findings and level of risk in each area. Auditors are required to score specific elements, such as documentation, personal protective equipment, and facility design, during the audit. These elements are scored using a dropdown list with options such as "Met," "Partially Met," and "Not Met." If an item is not applicable, select "N/A" from the dropdown list. The data will automatically calculate the total score achieved, as well as the overall percentage score for each facility area.

After completing the audit, auditors must provide the area in charge with a summary of findings, identifying necessary corrective actions. An audit score below the required threshold for a clinical area indicates that compliance is unsatisfactory and improvements are needed. Identified issues should be documented in an action plan, with deadlines for improving compliance. Each facility area has its own timeframes for rectifying compliance issues.
